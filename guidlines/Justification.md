Describe Response Strengths
Generate a list of strengths of the response. These strengths may touch on areas such as factual accuracy, coherence, relevance, readability, formatting, completeness, instruction following, verbosity, and other areas.
A high-quality strength should be:
Complete sentence: A standalone sentence starting with a clear subject like “The response...” or “The model...”
Objective: Based on something observable in the response, not opinion.
Specific: Clearly states what the response did well.
Relevant: Focused on something that improves correctness, clarity, reasoning, or instruction-following.
Self-contained: Understandable on its own without extra context.
When writing Strengths:
❌Do not include links used for fact-checking in the description
❌ Do not use first-person statements (e.g., “I think that...”)
❌ Do not past tense - ONLY use PRESENT tense
❌ Avoid vague statements or lack of specificity
❌ Write complete sentences with no grammatical or spelling errors
Each response strength should be written in its own text box. If a response is very poor, there may be no strengths to note. Most middle-of-the-road responses will have 2–4 strengths, while particularly strong responses may have five. You do not need to go beyond five.
Important: Strengths should focus on specific aspects of the response that meaningfully contribute to answering the user’s query. Do not list basic expectations that apply to nearly all acceptable responses, and do not combine strengths and weaknesses in the same statement unless brief contextualization is necessary.

Baseline Expectations and Strengths
Strengths must go beyond baseline expectations. Right now, the only criteria that should be treated as baseline and therefore not listed as strengths are:
Proper spelling and grammar
Understanding user intent
Responding in the correct language
All other positive qualities, including factual correctness or selecting the correct answer, may be listed as strengths when relevant to the prompt. Correctness is not automatically baseline, especially in tasks where arriving at the right answer is nontrivial.
In the very rare edge case where a response is extremely brief but still merits a top score, it is acceptable to reference a baseline expectation in order to assign a 5.
❌ “The Response has correct grammar and punctuation”

A suggested format of a Strength:
“The response [what it does well], [why it delivers meaningful value to the user]”
For example:
“The response provides a factually accurate answer by listing five must-see places in Paris in a concise, easy-to-scan format.”

Strengths: Best Practices and Examples
From Generic to Specific: Strength Statement Examples
When writing evaluations, avoid generic praise (e.g., “correctly answers the question” or “factually accurate”). Instead, make your feedback specific by explicitly stating what the response did well and how it addressed the user’s request.
For example:
Instead of: “The response correctly answers the question and provides an appropriate level of nuance,”
Write: “The response correctly explains the main causes of inflation and provides appropriate nuance regarding their relative importance.”
Instead of: “The response provides a factually accurate answer that directly addresses the user’s question,”
Write: “The response provides a factually accurate answer by listing five must-see places in Paris in a concise, easy-to-scan format.”
Instead of: “The response directly answers the user’s question and explains its reasoning clearly using multiple examples,”
Write: “The response directly answers the user’s question by arguing that Messi is the better player and supports this claim with multiple concrete examples of his achievements.”
Best Practices
Best Practices | Bad Example | Good Example

1. Ensure strengths are written as complete, standalone sentences with a clear subject
   ❌ “Correctly identifies A as the only correct answer.”
   ✅ “The response correctly identifies A as the only correct answer.”
2. Ensure the strength correctly characterizes the overall response quality
   ❌ “The response correctly describes all steps leading to the final result, which is also correct”
   when there is a mistake in one of the steps (that does not impact the final result)
   ✅ “The response includes all steps leading to the final result, and although one contains a mistake, it is still able to reach the correct number asked by the user.”
3. Ensure the strength is a relevant aspect of overall quality
   ❌ “The generated list is alphabetized.”
   when alphabetization is not explicitly requested by prompt, and doesn’t meaningfully improve the response’s readability
   ✅ “The use of bold headers and bullet points breaks down the detailed explanation into digestible sections, enhancing the overall readability of the text.”
4. Ensure the strength is free from subjectivity
   ❌ “The response includes a step-by-step approach that I think is very aesthetically pleasing.”
   ✅ “The response effectively describes complex machine learnings concepts using a clear, step-by-step approach.”
5. Ensure the strength is free from vague language, and includes the appropriate amount of specificity
   ❌ “The response is accurate.”
   ✅ “The response correctly lists the key differences between decision trees and random forests.”
6. Ensure the strength addresses all of the provided formatting requirements listed above.
   ❌ “The response is correct according to https://cloud.google.com/discover/what-is-agentic-coding.”
   This response includes tools/links used for validation.
   ✅ “The workflow described for developing agentic applications with enterprise data is factually correct.”
7. Ensure the strength highlights a specific contribution to addressing the user’s query, rather than basic expectations (e.g., grammar, spelling, or generic understanding of intent).
   ❌ “The grammar and spelling of the response are both completely correct.”
   ❌ “The response understands the user intent.”
   ✅ “The response clearly explains the trade-offs between supervised and unsupervised learning using concrete examples, which directly supports the user’s request for a comparison.”
8. Ensure the strength focuses only on what the response does well, and does not combine strengths and areas of improvement, except when brief contextualization is necessary.
   ❌ “The response lays out a clear chain of thought with a logical method for calculating the number of buckets but unfortunately makes one error that leads to an incorrect final answer.”
   ✅ “The response lays out a clear and logically structured approach for solving the problem.”

Describe Response Areas for Improvement
🧠 Helpful mental model
Areas of Improvement (AOIs) should capture all changes necessary for the response to achieve a perfect score. In other words, if every AOI were fully addressed and corrected, the revised response should meet all Guidelines and earn a 5/5 Overall Quality Score.
For each aspect of the response that requires improvement, clearly describe how the model did not meet expectations and why it matters to overall response quality.
Each Area of Improvement should be documented in its own text box. Depending on the response, you may identify no areas of improvement or several.
A high-quality area of improvement should be:
Complete sentence: A standalone sentence starting with a clear subject like “The response...” or “The model...”
Objective: Based on something observable in the response, not opinion.
Specific: Clearly states what the response lacked.
Relevant: Focused on something that improves correctness, clarity, reasoning, or instruction-following.
Self-contained: Understandable on its own without extra context (including the response excerpt)
When writing areas of improvement:
❌ Do not include tools or links used for fact-checking in the description
❌ Do not use first-person statements (e.g., “I think that...”)
❌ Do not past tense - ONLY use PRESENT tense
❌ Avoid vague statements or lack of specificity
❌ Write complete sentences with no grammatical or spelling errors
Important: Only publicly-accessible, non-paywalled sources should be used.

A suggested format of area of improvement:
“The response [what does not meet expectations / standards as described by the behavioral guidelines], [why does this degrade the usability of the response for the user ]”
For example:
“The response contains a clear grammatical error in the phrase “it readers can almost read it” which negatively impacts the quality of the advice.”
