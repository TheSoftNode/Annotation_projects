General Common Mistakes
• Evaluating response quality during task flagging. Task flagging is about categorizing the task, not judging whether a model response is good or bad.
• Skipping safety checks. Always evaluate safety-related flags first. If a task may elicit unsafe content or includes task-irrelevant PII, select the appropriate flag.
• Under-flagging expert domains. When in doubt about whether a task requires expert knowledge (e.g., STEM, legal, finance), err on the side of flagging.
• Selecting only one flag when multiple apply. You may select more than one task flag if applicable.
 ◦ Note: If it is a “high risk” topic such as financial, medical, legal, etc. that would require advice, but could potentially lead to an unsafe response - only mark the expertise and not as unsafe unless the topic is clearly malicious or puts someone in immediate harm (e.g: embezzlement or self-harm).
• Overthinking borderline cases. The guidelines are intentionally inclusive—flagging a task does not “invalidate” it; it helps route it correctly.
